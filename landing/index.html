०१ §01 · The problem

Your copy lives in a hundred places. And they all disagree.

The button says “Get started” in the doc, “Get Started!” in the mockup, and “Start now” in production. Nobody decided this. It simply… drifted.

Copy scattered across hundreds of frames. Stale strings shipped in code three sprints ago. A rogue typo that sailed past QA because the source of truth was a screenshot in someone’s DMs.

Three tools. Three versions. Zero agreement. That is the chaos Chitra was summoned to end.

चित्र

The mythology

Meet Chitra, the divine archivist reborn.

In Hindu myth, Chitragupta keeps a flawless, eternal record of every word and deed — nothing escapes his ledger. Chitra is that scribe reincarnated inside your canvas. She watches every string, links every component, and sees to it that the copy your writer drafts is the copy your designer sees and your developer ships.

No drift. No chaos. Only the record.

✦

०४ §04 · Get started

Summon her in four steps.

Chitra arrives as a development plugin. The ritual, in her own words:

  1. १

    Open Figma — the desktop app, if you please.

    “I do my finest work at a proper desk.”

  2. २

    Go to Plugins → Development → Import plugin from manifest…

    “A formal invitation. How civilized.”

  3. ३

    Select Chitra’s manifest.json

    “Point me to my papers. I shall take it from here.”

  4. ४

    Run Plugins → Development → Chitra

    “Consider your ledger open.”

Select a frame → she gathers every string → assign tokens, set statuses, edit once → export copy.json for your engineers.

She is named for Chitragupta — the archivist of the gods, keeper of the eternal record, from whom no word or deed slips away. Your product’s words deserve a keeper of the same rank: one ledger, faithfully kept, from first draft to shipped screen.
