# voice.md — the copy voice system

## Studio rules

- Lead with the noun. "Copy tokens sync from one file." Not "Experience seamless syncing."
- Active voice. Short sentences allowed. Vary sentence length — a long one, then a short one.
- Specifics over generics: numbers, filenames, real nouns. "One JSON file" beats "a single source."
- Matter-of-fact. State what it does; let the reader conclude it's good.
- Dry humor only in microcopy and footers, never in headlines.
- Contractions are fine.

## Banlist

seamless · effortless · powerful · robust · elevate · unlock · streamline · revolutionary · game-changing · AI-powered · crafted · curated · at your fingertips · really · very · truly · solution · platform · synergy · leverage · 10x

Also banned as rhythms (see anti-slop.md): gerund triads, "not just X — it's Y", "whether you're… or…", rhetorical-question openers, three-item parallel lists as default.

## Signature devices

- `//` mono eyebrow above headings: `// copy tokens`
- One italic-serif word per major heading, max: "Every string, *accounted* for."
- A footer signature line (see brand/chitra.md for Chitra's).

## The Indian-maximalist fusion rule

Copy is nonchalant, understated, deadpan-confident. The fonts and visuals carry the boldness — never the words. A giant vermilion headline set in a display serif says three plain words. If the visuals are quiet, the copy still doesn't get louder; pick a louder aesthetic instead.

## The two-register rule

A product may have a *persona* voice — mythic, characterful — but it lives only in in-product microcopy (loading states, empty states, confirmations). Marketing copy stays plain-register studio voice. Example: Chitra says "Order is restored." inside the plugin; the landing page says "Every string, accounted for." Never mix registers on one surface.
